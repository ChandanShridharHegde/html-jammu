Jammu and Kashmir

History:

Before 1947, the modern state of Jammu and Kashmir spanned 86,024 square miles, stretching between latitudes 32° 78' to 36° 58' N and longitudes 73° 27' to 80° 72' E. This vast region includedJammu, Ladakh, Gilgit, Hunza, Nagar, Punial, and Yasin. Chitral, a small state to the northwest of Gilgit, paid tribute to the ruler of Kashmir. The state's current form was largely shaped by the relentless efforts of, Maharaja Gulab Singh , who founded the Dogra Hindu dynasty in Kashmir in the second half of the 19th century.

Nestled in the Himalayas, Kashmir is often considered one of the most beautiful places on Earth. It is unique in India for having a recorded history of its ancient past, unlike many other regions of the country. This led the 11th-century Islamic scholar Al-Beruni to observe that Indians generally lacked a sense of history. However, Kashmir has produced notable historians, such as Kalhana who wrote the Rajatarangini, and Bilhana, a Sanskrit historian born in Kashmir who later became a court poet in South India and authored Vikramankadeva-charita to honor the reign of the Chalukya king, Vikramaditya VI.
The name Kashmir is derived from "Ka" (water) and "Shimeera" (to desiccate), meaning "land desiccated from water." According to tradition, Kashmir was once a lake, drained by the ancient Indian sage Kashyap. The region was part of theMauryan Empire under Emperor Ashoka, who is credited with founding the city ofSrinagar around 250 BC.

Major Cities Of Jammu and Kashmir:

The city of Jammu is named after Jambu Lochan , the brother of Bahu, a powerful local chieftain who ruled during the ninth century. It is said that on becoming king, Jambu Lochan went on a hunt and, crossing the Tawi, found a deer and a tiger drinking water from the same tank. His ministers explained that this meant that the soil of the place was so virtuous that no living creature bore enmity against another. Raja Jambu Lochan, decided to build his capital,Jambupura , on this soil, on the right bank of the Tawi overlooking his brother king Bahu's fort. Jambupura later came to be known as Jammu.
Jammu , the winter capital of Jammu and Kashmir union territory , is beautifully perched on the hillock, at the foothill of which flows river Tawi, often described as “Suryaputri” In holy Scriptures and historic books. Located between 32’44 North and 74’ 55’ East at 1200 feet above sea level, jammu city has expanded on both the banks of river Tawi but the gilded pinnacles of several temples still and to its striking view.

Srinagar city , summer capital of Jammu and Kashmir union territory , northern India, situated in the Kashmir region of the Indian subcontinent. The city lies along the banks of the Jhelum River at an elevation of 5,200 feet (1,600 metres) in the Vale of Kashmir.
The earliest historical records, including Kalhana's"Rajatarangini" , mention the Sanskrit name "Shri-nagara, which scholars have interpreted in two distinct ways. One interpretation is "The City of Shri" (श्री), referring to the Hindu goddess of wealth, Lakshmi, hence meaning "City of Lakshmi." The other interpretation is "Surya-nagar", meaning "City of the Sun," associating it with the Sun god, Surya.

Muzaffarabad is a city in the region of Jammu and Kashmir and serves as the capital of Azad Kashmir. It is the largest city in the area, located within the broader, historically disputed region of Kashmir. The city holds administrative importance and is known for its geographical position, nestled between mountain ranges, with the rivers Jhelum and Neelum flowing through it.

Leh, town, Ladakh union territory, northern India. The town is located in the valley of the upper Indus River at an elevation of 11,550 feet (3,520 meters), surrounded by the towering peaks of theLadakh Range (a southeastern extension of the Karakoram Range).

Kargil is a city in the Indian-administered union territory of Ladakh, situated in the Kashmir region. It serves as the joint capital of Ladakh, alongside Leh, and is the administrative center of the Kargil district. Kargil is the second-largest city in Ladakh after Leh and is located 204 kilometers (127 miles) east of Srinagar in Jammu and Kashmir, and 234 kilometers (145 miles) west of Leh. The city is positioned on the bank of theSuru River, near its confluence with the Wakha Rong river, which provides the most accessible route to Leh.


Gilgit is a city in the region of Gilgit-Baltistan of Jammu and Kashmir, located within the broader, historically disputed Kashmir area. It is the capital of the Gilgit-Baltistan region, situated in a wide valley near the confluence of theGilgit and Hunza rivers. Known for its stunning natural beauty, Gilgit is a major destination for tourists, serving as a hub for trekking and mountaineering expeditions in the Karakoram mountain range.
